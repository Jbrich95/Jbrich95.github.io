Jordan Richards

I am a PhD graduate from the STOR-i centre for doctoral training at Lancaster university. I also completed both my undergraduate and postgraduate masters at Lancaster.

I am mainly interested in extreme value theory and spatial statistics. In particular, my interests lie in the statistical modelling of extreme weather and climate events. My PhD thesis concerned the extremal behaviour of aggregates of spatial processes and was co-supervised by Jonathan Tawn, Jennifer Wadsworth, both of Lancaster university, and Simon Brown of the Hadley Centre at the UK Met Office.

I currently reside at King Abdullah University of Science and Technology (KAUST) in Saudia Arabia, where I am a postdoctoral fellow of statistics. My postdoc research focus is on high-dimensional modelling of spatial extremes.

Software

pinnEV. R package for fitting extreme value (and other) regression models using the partially-interpretable neural network (PINN) framework proposed by Richards and Huser (2022). Currently in development.
scePrecip. R code for fitting spatial conditional extremes models to censored data, e.g., precipitation. Accompanies Richards, Tawn and Brown (2022)
sdfExtreme. R package for performing spatial deformations to handle nonstationarity in spatial extremal dependence. Accompanies Richards and Wadsworth (2021)
CondExtremesPy. Functions for fitting Heffernan and Tawn (2004) conditional extremes models in Python (Ongoing) INARMA_RJMCMC_R/INARMA_RJMCMC_py. R/Python code for reversible jump MCMC to determine order and estimate parameters of INARMA models
